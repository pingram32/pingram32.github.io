ITCP 1
Intro to Competitive Programming
Reccomended: Some familiarity with any coding language. The ability to apply critical thinking to solve abstract problems.
Comfortable in math.
Textbook: USACO Guide
Course Description: Examines fundamental topics in Computer Programming such as standard input/output, data structures, time
complexity, and algorithms using C++. Students will learn how to apply these topics to solve problems similar to those seen in
Competitive Programming competitions. Course content can be found on code-e.org.
Supplemental Assignments: There will be 2 supplemetal assignments assigned for each topic resulting in a total of 12 supplemental
assignments.
In Class Assignments: Each class session will have 2 in-class assignments. After each lecture students will be given time to work
on these assignments and ask any questions they may have.
Lectures: Every Tuesday and Thursday from 5pm to 6pm lecture will be held. You will be given 1 hour after lecture to complete the
in class assignments.
Final Competition: For the final week we will host a programming competition amongst the class. Prizes will be awarded for
placement and other outstanding achievements during the course.
Attendance and Behavior: Attendance is reccomended if you wish to do well and is required to qualify for the prizes handed out
after the final competition. Appropriated behavior and online courtesy is expected in Discord call.
Academic Honesty: Cheating will not be tolerated and will result in a zero on the graded item. Violation of the academic honesty
policy will result in disqualification from any final awards.
Student Learning Outcomes: Upon successful completion of the course, students will have:
Learned the fundamentals of Computer Programming using C++
Developed the ability to understand mathematic and Computer Programming abstractions
Strengthened their problem solving ability
